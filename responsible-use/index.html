Signal Bench — Responsible Use

Last updated: 2026-07-01

What Signal Bench does

Signal Bench is a passive field telemetry tool. When you start a Walk Capture, it records what your phone observes from its built-in sensors:

  • How many nearby Bluetooth advertisements your phone detected
  • How many nearby Wi-Fi networks were broadcasting
  • Cellular signal type and strength
  • Motion and environmental conditions
  • Location fix quality

It does this passively — it does not connect to any device or network, does not inspect content, and does not transmit data.

What Signal Bench cannot do

  • Cannot identify people. Radio signal counts do not map to individuals.
  • Cannot prove events. Observations are snapshots of ambient conditions, not evidence of any intent or cause.
  • Cannot detect threats. Signal Bench has no threat model. "More signals" is not a warning.
  • Cannot tell you if someone is surveilling you. Nearby Bluetooth or Wi-Fi activity is normal in any populated area.
  • Cannot be used as evidence. Signal Bench results are not forensic data, are not authenticated, and are not admissible in any proceeding.

Appropriate uses

Signal Bench is appropriate for:

  • Personal curiosity about ambient signal environments
  • Learning how your phone's sensors work
  • Informal comparison of signal conditions between locations
  • Hobbyist RF exploration
  • Testing your own network environment (on property you control)
  • Debugging sensor availability on specific hardware
  • Open-source software development and research

Inappropriate uses

Do not use Signal Bench to:

  • Surveil, track, monitor, or follow another person without their knowledge and explicit consent
  • Harass or stalk any individual
  • Compile profiles of nearby individuals based on signal patterns
  • Draw conclusions about specific people from signal observations
  • Use session results to accuse, implicate, or report a specific person
  • Present session results as evidence in any legal, employment, or safety context
  • Scan environments you do not own or have permission to be in for purposes beyond your own curiosity
  • Use in professional surveillance, investigation, or law enforcement workflows

A note on passive observation

Walking in a public place and observing ambient signals is analogous to looking around. Signal Bench records counts and signal strengths — general environmental data. This is not the same as recording, photographing, or identifying individuals.

However, context matters. Using a tool repeatedly to build a pattern of observations about a specific person's home, workplace, or routine — even passively — can constitute harassment or stalking regardless of the tool's technical capabilities.

Use Signal Bench for your own learning and exploration. If a use case involves monitoring another person, stop and do not proceed.

Data handling reminder

Signal Bench is designed to keep data on your device. When you export data, you are responsible for how you handle and share the export file. Sharing detailed session exports publicly or with third parties without considering the privacy of any people who may have been nearby during the session is your responsibility.

The default Redacted export mode removes identifiers. Use it unless you have a specific technical reason to need the Detailed export.

Contact

For questions about appropriate use, contact the project maintainer via the contact information in the Google Play Store listing.
