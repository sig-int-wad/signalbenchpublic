Signal Walk — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-07-03

Overview

Signal Walk is a local-first passive field telemetry tool for Android. When you start a Walk Capture, it records what your phone's built-in sensors observe — nearby signal counts, measurements, motion, environmental conditions, and location quality — and stores sessions, observations, insights, and reports locally on your device.

Your session data never leaves your device. However, the app's map view and AI features make outbound network requests to third-party services, which are described in detail below.

Information the developer collects

The Signal Walk developer does not operate an account system, session-data backend, or cloud session storage.

No session data or sensor observations are transmitted to or stored by the Signal Walk developer. The bundled Google ML Kit SDK separately collects limited device/app information, identifiers, performance and feature-event metrics, and errors for Google's diagnostics and usage analytics, as described below.

On-device session data

When you start a Walk Capture, Signal Walk reads from your device's sensors and stores the following locally:

  • Counts and signal strength of nearby Bluetooth advertisements (not device contents)
  • Counts and signal strength of nearby Wi-Fi network broadcasts (not network content or traffic)
  • Cellular network type and signal strength (not call content or contacts)
  • Motion readings from accelerometer and gyroscope
  • Environmental readings from pressure, light, proximity, and magnetic field sensors
  • Location quality indicators (fix availability and accuracy; precise coordinates are not stored by default)

This data is stored only in the app's private directory on your device. You can delete it at any time from within the app.

Identifier masking

By default, Signal Walk masks hardware identifiers before storing them:

  • Bluetooth device addresses are stored in masked form (e.g., ••:••:XX)
  • Wi-Fi BSSIDs are stored in masked form (e.g., ••:••:XX)
  • Precise GPS coordinates are not stored in Redacted or Summary mode

If you choose Detailed export mode, a warning is shown before the export is written explaining what additional data will be included.

Third-party network requests

Although Signal Walk does not transmit your session data, its map and AI libraries make outbound requests. Map requests expose an IP address and requested viewport to the configured tile provider. ML Kit model delivery and SDK diagnostics contact Google; SDK diagnostics may include device/app information and identifiers. Neither path sends Signal Walk session content or sensor observations.

Map tile downloads (MapLibre / OpenFreeMap)

Signal Walk includes an offline field map powered by MapLibre, an open-source mapping SDK. Online maps are off by default; the map view starts in field-grid/offline mode. Only after you explicitly opt in via the in-app disclosure does MapLibre fetch map tiles from the configured tile server. By default this is OpenFreeMap (tiles.openfreemap.org).

Each tile request may include your device's IP address (as with any HTTP request), requested tiles and map resources, and viewport information that may reveal the approximate area of the map you are viewing. OpenFreeMap's privacy practices are governed by their own privacy policy. Signal Walk does not receive or store any information from these tile requests. Map tiles are cached on-device for offline use.

To avoid tile requests entirely, do not open the map view, or use the app in airplane mode after tiles have been cached.

Signal Walk does not send session recordings or sensor histories to the map provider. If you choose to open another maps application, that application handles the request under its own privacy practices.

AI model download (ML Kit Gemini Nano)

Signal Walk uses Google's ML Kit to run on-device AI analysis of session data, backed by Google AICore (the system service that hosts the Gemini Nano model). AI summaries are user-initiated: the first time you tap "Generate insights" and acknowledge the in-app AI disclosure, the Gemini Nano model may be downloaded to your device. This download is handled by Google's ML Kit infrastructure and is governed by Google's Privacy Policy. Because this feature depends on Google AICore, Signal Walk requires a device with Vulkan 1.0 graphics support; devices without it will not see Signal Walk as installable on Google Play.

The model download is initiated by AI feature use. AI inference is intended to run on-device through Google ML Kit/AICore with no cloud fallback, and prompt and session content is not sent to a Signal Walk developer-operated server or to Google for AI inference. Google ML Kit may still contact Google for model or compatibility updates and may collect SDK diagnostics and usage information — such as device and application information, installation/device or similar identifiers, SDK/API/model/feature configuration, feature events, performance and reliability measurements, input/output size metadata, error codes, and configured language — for diagnostics and usage measurement. This is Google SDK diagnostics, not advertising or developer-added behavioral analytics, and is encrypted in transit. Signal Walk cannot delete information retained by Google. You can disable AI summaries at any time from the app drawer; declining or disabling does not affect sessions, exports, or maps.

Information we do not collect

  • We do not collect your name, email, or any account information
  • We do not collect precise GPS coordinates (in any default mode)
  • We do not collect the content of any Bluetooth device or Wi-Fi network
  • We do not collect call records, contacts, or messages
  • We do not collect photos, videos, or audio
  • We do not track your location across sessions
  • We do not use an advertising SDK or the Android Advertising ID, and we do not sell personal information
  • We do not add crash-reporting services or developer behavioral analytics; Google ML Kit includes its own disclosed diagnostics and usage measurement
  • We do not collect background location, microphone data, or camera data
  • We do not pair with, connect to, advertise as, spoof, jam, or intercept Bluetooth devices
  • We do not join Wi-Fi networks or intercept Wi-Fi traffic
  • We do not read data from other apps on your device
  • We do not receive or store any data from map tile requests
  • We do not receive or store any data from the ML Kit model download

How your session data is used

Your on-device session data is used only to:

  1. Display session results to you within the app
  2. Generate exports that you explicitly request
  3. Provide on-device AI insights (processed locally; data does not leave your device for AI inference)

Signal Walk does not use your session data to train AI models, improve any external service, target advertising, or for any other purpose beyond displaying your own results to you.

Data sharing

Signal Walk does not share your session data with any third party. Android sharing occurs only after you explicitly export or share. Export files remain wherever you save or share them until you delete them under the controls provided by that destination. We have no visibility into what you share or with whom.

Data storage and deletion

Session data is stored in the app's private directory on your device (context.filesDir/sessions/). This data:

  • Is not accessible to other apps without your device being rooted
  • Is deleted when you uninstall Signal Walk
  • Can be deleted at any time from within the app

Note on Android Auto Backup: Signal Walk declares android:allowBackup="false" in its manifest. Auto Backup and ADB backup are both fully disabled — no session data, exports, or app preferences are ever included in any on-device or cloud backup.

How to delete your data

  • Delete one session: Sessions tab → tap session → overflow menu (⋮) → "Delete session…" → confirm
  • Delete all sessions: Sessions tab → overflow menu (⋮) → "Delete all sessions…" → confirm
  • Delete a supported cached map region: Open the map's offline-cache controls → "Delete cached region"

These actions delete the supported local app data from your device. Export files you have already saved outside the app must be deleted separately.

Children's privacy

Signal Walk is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect any information from children.

Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above and note significant changes in the Play Store release notes.
