Privacy

Signal Walk Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 3, 2026

Privacy at a glance

  • Signal Walk does not require an account.
  • Saved session content remains in the app's private storage unless you export or share it.
  • The developer does not operate a backend that receives or stores your sessions.
  • Online map imagery and Google model delivery are optional, disclosed network features.
  • Google ML Kit may collect limited SDK diagnostics and usage information as described below.

Overview

Signal Walk is a local-first Android field telemetry tool. When you start a Walk Capture, the app organizes wireless and sensor readings available through your phone into a session you can review, save, and export.

Signal Walk does not automatically upload your session content. Saved sessions stay on your device unless you choose to export or share a file. Optional map and AI-related features can make third-party network requests, which are explained in this policy.

Information received by the developer

The Signal Walk developer does not operate an account system, session-data backend, or cloud session-storage service.

The developer does not receive or store your Signal Walk sessions or sensor observations. The bundled Google ML Kit SDK may separately collect limited device and app information, identifiers, feature events, performance measurements, and errors for Google's diagnostics and usage measurement. Those requests are described under Third-party network requests.

Data stored on your device

When you start a Walk Capture, Signal Walk may read and store the following information locally, depending on your device, Android version, settings, and permissions:

  • Counts and signal strength of nearby Bluetooth advertisements, not Bluetooth device contents
  • Counts and signal strength of nearby Wi-Fi broadcasts, not network traffic or content
  • Cellular network type and signal strength, not call content or contacts
  • Motion readings from sensors such as the accelerometer and gyroscope
  • Environmental readings from available pressure, light, proximity, and magnetic-field sensors
  • Location-quality indicators such as fix availability and accuracy
  • Session metadata, observations, events, insights, and generated reports

This information is stored in the app's private directory on your device and can be deleted from within the app.

Identifier masking and location detail

Signal Walk is designed to reduce sensitive detail by default:

  • Bluetooth device addresses are stored in masked form, such as ••:••:XX.
  • Wi-Fi BSSIDs are stored in masked form, such as ••:••:XX.
  • Precise GPS coordinates are not stored in Redacted or Summary-only mode.

If you choose Detailed export mode, Signal Walk shows a warning before writing the file and explains the additional detail that will be included.

Third-party network requests

Signal Walk does not send session content to a developer-operated server. However, optional map features and Google's ML Kit infrastructure can make network requests to third parties.

Map tiles: MapLibre and OpenFreeMap

Signal Walk uses MapLibre, an open-source mapping SDK. Online map imagery is off by default, and the map begins in a field-grid or offline mode. Map tiles are requested only after you opt in through the in-app disclosure. The default tile provider is OpenFreeMap at tiles.openfreemap.org.

As with any web request, a tile provider can receive your device's IP address. It can also receive the requested tiles and map resources, which may indicate the approximate area you are viewing. The provider handles that information under its own privacy practices.

Signal Walk does not receive or store information from those tile requests and does not send session recordings or sensor histories to the map provider. Tiles may be cached on your device for offline use.

To avoid map-tile requests, keep online maps disabled. You can also use airplane mode after a supported region has been cached.

On-device AI: Google ML Kit, AICore, and Gemini Nano

Signal Walk uses Google's ML Kit and AICore to provide supported on-device AI analysis. AI summaries are user-initiated. The first time you choose Generate insights and accept the in-app disclosure, Google's infrastructure may download a compatible Gemini Nano model to your device. That delivery is governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

Signal Walk is designed to perform AI inference on-device without a cloud-inference fallback. Prompt and session content is not sent to a Signal Walk developer-operated server or to Google for AI inference.

Google ML Kit may still contact Google for model delivery, compatibility information, or updates. The SDK may collect diagnostics and usage information such as device and application information, installation/device or similar identifiers, SDK and model configuration, feature events, performance and reliability measurements, input/output size metadata, error codes, and configured language. This is Google SDK diagnostics and usage measurement, not advertising or developer-added behavioral analytics, and Google states that this data is encrypted in transit.

Signal Walk cannot delete information retained by Google. You can decline or disable AI summaries without affecting normal session capture, exports, or maps.

Because this feature depends on Google AICore, Signal Walk requires compatible device support, including Vulkan 1.0 graphics support. Devices that do not meet the declared requirement may not be offered the app through Google Play.

Information Signal Walk does not collect

  • Your name, email address, or account information
  • Precise GPS coordinates in any default mode
  • The content of Bluetooth devices, Wi-Fi networks, or network traffic
  • Call records, contacts, messages, photos, videos, or audio
  • Background location, microphone data, or camera data
  • Data from other apps on your device
  • The Android Advertising ID

Signal Walk does not include an advertising SDK, does not sell personal information, and does not add a developer-operated behavioral analytics or crash-reporting service. Google ML Kit's separately disclosed diagnostics and usage measurement still apply.

Signal Walk does not pair with, join, advertise as, spoof, jam, or intercept nearby Bluetooth devices or Wi-Fi networks.

How local session data is used

Your on-device session data is used to:

  1. Display live and saved session results in the app
  2. Create exports that you explicitly request
  3. Generate supported on-device AI insights that you initiate

Signal Walk does not use your session content to train AI models, target advertising, or improve a developer-operated external service.

Exports and sharing

Signal Walk creates an export only after you request one. Android's sharing interface is opened only after you choose to export or share. The developer cannot see what you export, where you save it, or who receives it.

Once an export leaves the app, it remains under the storage, sharing, retention, and deletion controls of the destination you selected. Delete exported files separately when you no longer need them.

Storage, backup, and deletion

Session data is stored in the app's private directory at context.filesDir/sessions/. It is not normally accessible to other apps, is removed when you uninstall Signal Walk, and can be deleted from within the app.

Android backup is disabled. Signal Walk declares android:allowBackup="false". Android Auto Backup and ADB backup are disabled, so session data, app preferences, and app-managed exports are not included in those backup systems.

Delete data from the app

  • Delete one session: Sessions → open the session → overflow menu (⋮) → Delete session… → confirm
  • Delete all sessions: Sessions → overflow menu (⋮) → Delete all sessions… → confirm
  • Delete a supported cached map region: Open the map's offline-cache controls → Delete cached region

Files already exported outside Signal Walk must be deleted from the location where you saved or shared them.

Children's privacy

Signal Walk is not directed to children under 13, and the developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the date at the top of the page will be updated. Significant changes may also be noted in Google Play release information.
